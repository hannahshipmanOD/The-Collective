## State-Specific Deep Dives

### Texas (TX) — Option Period

- **Unique feature:** Option period (7-10 days), separate from diligence. Buyer can walk for ANY reason during option period — only forfeits option fee (typically nominal).
- **Option fee:** Separate from earnest money. Non-refundable to seller if buyer walks during option.
- **Diligence period:** Runs concurrently with option period.
- **Promulgated forms:** Texas uses TREC (Texas Real Estate Commission) promulgated forms — state-regulated, cannot modify.
- **Title insurance:** Promulgated rates — no shopping for title insurance rates.
- **No transfer tax:** Texas has no deed transfer tax or mortgage tax.
- **COE timing:** 2 weeks minimum (DFW homebuilder markets may be 2 wks).
- **Markets:** DFW, HOU, ATX, SAT, KLN, CRP

### Florida (FL) — Documentary Tax + HOA

- **Documentary tax:** Statewide. Miami-Dade has additional surtax.
- **Intangible tax:** On mortgages.
- **HOA estoppel letters:** Required for any property with HOA — can take 10-15 business days.
- **Closing disclosure:** Must be received 3 business days before close.
- **Attorney not always required:** Title company can close in most counties, but attorney often involved.
- **Markets:** MIA, TPA, ORL, JAX, SWF

### Georgia (GA) — Attorney State

- **Attorney required:** All closings must go through a Georgia-licensed attorney. Plan for longer timelines.
- **Real Estate Transfer Tax:** Seller typically pays.
- **Intangible Recording Tax:** On mortgages — buyer pays.
- **Security Deed:** Georgia uses "Security Deed" (aka Deed to Secure Debts) instead of Deed of Trust or Mortgage.
- **Closing posture:** Funding-based.
- **Markets:** ATL (primary), portions of CHA market

### North Carolina (NC) — Due Diligence Period

- **Due diligence period:** Negotiated in contract (not statutory). Earnest money is at risk AFTER DD period expires.
- **Due diligence fee:** Separate from earnest money. Non-refundable to seller.
- **Attorney required:** Yes — attorney must oversee closing.
- **Excise tax:** Plus 1% additional transfer tax in some counties (Dare, Currituck).
- **Markets:** CLT, RDU, GSO, AVL

### Colorado (CO) — HOA Review Period

- **Title commitment review period:** Buyer reviews title commitment for issues.
- **HOA document review period:** Buyer reviews HOA docs, can terminate if unacceptable.
- **Documentary Transfer Tax:** Yes, but varies by county.
- **Not an attorney state:** Title company handles closing.
- **Markets:** DEN, COS, NCO

### California (CA) — Documentary Transfer Tax + Local

- **Documentary Transfer Tax:** State-level, plus many cities/counties have additional local transfer taxes.
- **Grant Deed:** California uses Grant Deed (not Warranty Deed).
- **Deed of Trust:** Standard encumbrance instrument.
- **Recording-based closing:** Escrow company handles.
- **Title insurance:** Rates vary by county — no state promulgation.
- **City inspections:** Possible in LAX market.
- **Markets:** LAX, RIV, SAC, SAN, SFO

### Nevada (NV) — Real Property Transfer Tax

- **Real Property Transfer Tax:** Yes, flat rate.
- **Grant Deed:** Standard conveyance instrument (not Warranty Deed).
- **Deed of Trust:** Standard (Mortgage rare).
- **Recording-based closing:** Escrow company handles.
- **Markets:** LAS, RNO

### Ohio (OH) — Real Property Conveyance Fee

- **Real Property Conveyance Fee:** Varies by county.
- **Not attorney required:** Title company handles.
- **City inspections:** Possible in CLE market.
- **Title channel:** `#oh-title-osn`
- **Markets:** CIN, CLE, CMH

### Alabama (AL) — Deed Tax

- **Deed Tax:** Yes.
- **Mortgage Tax:** Yes.
- **Closing entity:** Title company, approved attorney, or bank.
- **Title channel:** `#al-title-osn`
- **Markets:** BHM

### Missouri/Kansas (MO/KS)

- **Missouri:** No deed transfer tax. Filed risk rates for title insurance. Deed of Trust (not Mortgage). Split closings possible.
- **Kansas:** No deed transfer tax but has Mortgage Registration Tax. Filed title rates.
- **Title channel:** `#moks-title-apex`
- **Markets:** KCI (Kansas City), STL (St. Louis — MO side)
